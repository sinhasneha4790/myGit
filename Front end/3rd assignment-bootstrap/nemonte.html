Nemonte Nenquimo Biography

A WAORANI LEADER and winner of the Goldman environmental prize

Nemonte Nenquimo is an Indigenous leader of the Waorani peoples in Ecuador. She is a founder of the non-profits Ceibo Alliance and Amazon Frontlines, both of which strive to defend Indigenous rights, culture, and land. Nenquimo has been instrumental in supporting calls to end oil extraction in the Amazon, and in August 2023 Ecuador successfully voted to block oil drilling in the Yasuní National Park.

In 2015, Nenquimo co-founded the indigenous-led nonprofit organization, Ceibo Alliance, to protect indigenous lands and in 2018, she was elected the first female president of CONCONAWEP, the Waorani organization of Pastaza province in Ecuador. In 2019, Nenquimo led her people’s historic legal victory against the Ecuadorian government, protecting half-a-million acres of primary rainforest from oil drilling, and setting a precedent for indigenous rights throughout the region.

In 2020, Nemonte received a grant to do a project that seeks to pilot Intercultural education schooling interventions in two Waorani communities, and was nominated as one of TIME's The 100 Most Influential People of 2020.
